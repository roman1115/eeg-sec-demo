EEG Speech Emotion Conversion Demo

Comparison of Speech Emotion Conversion Models Based on EEG Emotional Features (Audio Sample Demonstration)

Abstract

Existing speech emotion conversion methods have been widely studied for generating emotionally expressive speech. In parallel, electroencephalography (EEG) has shown potential in emotion-related tasks due to its ability to reflect neural activity. However, the integration of EEG signals into speech emotion conversion remains relatively underexplored. This study investigates the feasibility of incorporating EEG signals as an emotional conditioning modality for speech emotion conversion. A cross-modal framework is developed to integrate EEG-conditioned emotional representations into the speech generation process, where a staged training strategy is adopted to progressively align EEG and speech representations. In particular, structured temporal modeling is introduced to capture the temporal patterns of EEG signals, thereby enabling the learning of informative emotional representations for guiding speech generation. Experiments are conducted on a dataset with a limited set of basic emotional categories under controlled conditions. The results demonstrate that EEG signals can provide effective emotional guidance for speech generation, producing perceptually plausible emotional expression while maintaining speech naturalness. These findings suggest that EEG signals have the potential to serve as continuous emotional control inputs for speech emotion conversion and highlight a promising direction for cross-modal emotional speech generation.
