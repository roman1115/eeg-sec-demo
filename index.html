EEG Speech Emotion Conversion Demo

Comparison of Speech Emotion Conversion Models Based on EEG Emotional Features (Audio Sample Demonstration)

Abstract

To address the problem that existing speech emotion conversion methods mainly rely on speech signal modeling and struggle to accurately reflect the speaker's true emotional state, we propose a speech emotion conversion method guided by electroencephalography (EEG). This method introduces the emotional information contained in EEG into the speech emotion conversion process through cross-modal knowledge distillation and a conditional generation mechanism. First, the baseline speech emotion conversion model SGEVC is trained on the ESD dataset to ensure stable emotion conversion capability. Then, using SGEVC's speech emotion encoder as a teacher model on the EAV dataset, the EEG emotion extractor is guided to learn representations consistent with speech emotion features, and joint optimization is performed through regression and classification losses. Finally, the trained EEG emotion extraction module is integrated into SGEVC to construct an EEG-conditioned speech emotion conversion model. Experimental results show that this method enhances the consistency and accuracy of speech emotion expression while maintaining the naturalness and intelligibility of speech, validating the effectiveness of EEG-assisted speech emotion modeling.
