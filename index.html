EEG Speech Emotion Conversion Demo

Comparison of Speech Emotion Conversion Models Based on EEG Emotional Features (Audio Sample Demonstration)

Abstract

Speech emotion conversion aims to transform the emotional state of the source speech into a target emotion while keeping the speech content and speaker identity unchanged. It has important application value in fields such as human-computer interaction, affective computing, and assistive communication. Existing methods mainly rely on the speech signal itself for emotion modeling, making it difficult to accurately capture the true internal emotional state of the speaker, especially when emotional expression is inadequate or the speech is affected by noise.

To address this, this paper proposes a brain signal-guided speech emotion conversion framework based on EEG. Through cross-modal knowledge distillation and conditional generative models, the emotional information contained in EEG is effectively integrated into the speech emotion conversion process. This method employs a three-stage training strategy: first, a baseline speech emotion conversion model, SGEVC, is trained on the ESD dataset to establish stable emotion conversion capability; second, on the EAV dataset, the speech emotion encoder in SGEVC is used as a teacher model to guide the EEG emotion extractor to learn representations consistent with speech emotion features, optimized jointly with regression loss and classification loss; finally, the trained EEG emotion extraction module is embedded into the SGEVC model to construct an EEG-conditioned speech emotion conversion model, improving the accuracy and consistency of emotional expression while maintaining speech naturalness and intelligibility.
